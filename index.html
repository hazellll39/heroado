Project 01 · Featured

Model Rocketry
Design & Launch

✦ 3rd Place · Most Innovative Design

Led the full aerodynamic design cycle for a team-built model rocket. Introduced a dimpled nose cone concept borrowed from golf ball physics to reduce pressure drag — earning the competition's innovation award.

OpenRocketOnshapeSolidWorks3D PrintingLaser CuttingTelemetry

What I built & what I proved

Ran iterative OpenRocket simulations across 6 fin geometries and 3 nose cone profiles. All parts split between 3D-printed components (nose cone, boat tail) and laser-cut ply (fins, body tube slots). Post-launch telemetry extracted aero coefficients and validated predictions.

Telemetry-to-simulation agreement within 8% on drag coefficient — design validated
Dimpled nose cone won Most Innovative Design out of the full competition field
Designed payload bay, shock cord routing, and parachute deployment sequence
Coordinated fab tasks across a 5-person team under hard competition deadline
Project 02

Carbon Fibre
Glider Design

✦ 2nd Place · Furthest Flight Distance

Designed and hand-built a competition glider optimised for maximum distance. Chose CFRP for the wing and fuselage to hit an extreme stiffness-to-weight ratio — the aircraft that flew furthest of the entire field.

CFRP Composite LayupWing AerodynamicsStructural Fabrication
02
Engineering decisions

Airfoil selection driven by glide ratio modelling — targeting highest L/D within the competition weight class. Composite layup involved fibre orientation scheduling, vacuum bagging, cure cycle management, and post-cure trimming to final geometry.

Achieved the longest flight distance in the entire competition
CFRP wing delivered target stiffness under 60 g structural mass
Completed full fabrication cycle: layup → vacuum bag → cure → trim → test flight
Documented manufacturing process and failure modes for future iteration
Project 03

Landing Gear
System Design

Full landing gear assembly designed from scratch in SolidWorks and stress-tested in ANSYS under realistic load cases. Modal analysis identified critical natural frequencies before any physical build.

SolidWorksANSYS FEAModal AnalysisTechnical Docs
03
Analysis scope

Applied 2.5g hard-landing and side-load cases across the full assembly, using mesh convergence studies to confirm accuracy. Vibrational analysis revealed two modes within the operating band — geometry iterated to shift them outside the excitation range.

Von Mises stress and safety factors evaluated across all structural members
Modal analysis found 4 natural frequencies; 2 critical modes detuned via geometry iteration
Full technical documentation produced: drawings, FEA reports, assembly instructions
Demonstrated industry-standard simulation workflow end-to-end

Project 05

Walking Robot

CATIA V5MATLABLaser CuttingKinematics

From equations to walking hardware

Built a MATLAB kinematic model of the linkage, parameterised over 6 link lengths, ran constrained optimisation to minimise foot-path deviation. Winning geometry modelled in CATIA V5 and exported to DXF for laser cutting.

MATLAB optimisation reduced foot-path deviation by 34% vs baseline geometry
Full 3D assembly modelled in CATIA V5 with clearance analysis before fabrication
All links laser cut from 3mm acrylic; gear train assembled from standard components
Robot walked continuously under hand-crank input — zero motor, full kinematic transmission
